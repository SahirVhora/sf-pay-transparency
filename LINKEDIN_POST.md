## Post

I’ve been exploring what EU Pay Transparency readiness looks like inside SAP SuccessFactors.

The hard part is not just knowing the Directive exists.

The hard part is answering practical questions like:

- Are pay ranges actually maintained and mapped to jobs or positions?
- Is job architecture consistent enough to support same/equal-value analysis?
- Can compensation, gender, employment, position, and job data be joined cleanly?
- Can applicants see pay range information at the right stage?
- Can workers request their pay level and average pay levels by sex for the relevant category?
- Can HR, Reward, Legal, People Analytics, and worker representatives review the same evidence trail?

So I built a small local prototype: an SF Pay Transparency Readiness Checker.

It combines a workshop-style readiness checklist with optional local OData metadata checks, SuccessFactors configuration paths, a remediation roadmap, and a prototype evidence pack for Article 9-style analysis where the required joins are available.

It is not legal advice, not a certified compliance product, and not a production pay equity engine.

But as a discussion starter for SuccessFactors, HRIS, Reward, and People Analytics teams, I think this is where the work gets interesting: translating regulation into system readiness, data quality, workflow ownership, and evidence.

The EU Pay Transparency Directive deadline is getting close. For many organisations, the useful question now is not “have we read the regulation?” but “can our systems prove what we think they can prove?”

I’ve shared the prototype here:

https://github.com/SahirVhora/sf-pay-transparency

Curious how other SuccessFactors and Reward teams are approaching this. Are you starting with policy, data quality, job architecture, reporting, or workflow design?
